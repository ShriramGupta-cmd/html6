Database Recovery Techniques

Ensuring Consistency After Failures

Introduction

Database Recovery Techniques are essential for restoring database integrity after system crashes, power failures, or hardware malfunctions. These techniques ensure that data remains consistent and durable even when unexpected failures occur.

Why Recovery is Needed

Failures such as system crashes or transaction errors can corrupt a database. Recovery techniques help bring the database back to a stable state by undoing incomplete transactions and redoing completed ones.

Types of Failures

  • Transaction Failure: A specific transaction fails due to logical errors or system crashes.
  • System Crash: The database management system stops abruptly due to power or hardware issues.
  • Disk Failure: Physical damage to the storage medium.

Recovery Techniques

  • Log-Based Recovery: Maintains logs of all transactions. Uses Undo and Redo operations based on the log.
  • Checkpointing: Periodically saves the current state of the database to reduce recovery time.
  • Shadow Paging: Keeps a copy (shadow) of the database pages before modifying them.

Undo and Redo Operations

  • Undo: Reverses the effects of a transaction that did not complete successfully.
  • Redo: Reapplies the effects of a completed transaction to ensure durability.

Advantages

  • Maintains consistency and durability.
  • Quick recovery after crashes.
  • Log-based methods are reliable and efficient.

Conclusion

Database recovery techniques form the backbone of reliable database systems. By using logs, checkpoints, and undo/redo operations, they ensure that data is safe and recoverable even after critical failures.
